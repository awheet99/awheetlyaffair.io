Orange Pecan Date Nut Bread with Orange Cream Cheese Spread

Author: Ann Wheet
Recipe type: quick bread
Prep time: 40 mins
Cook time: 45 mins
Total time: 1 hour 25 mins
Serves: 10-12 slices

Bread Ingredients

  • 1 cup pitted dates, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup boiling fresh squeeze orange juice (2 large oranges) (NOTE: You'll need an 2 tablespoons of juice for cream cheese spread.
  • Zest from 1 orange
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature, plus 1 teaspoon for greasing pan
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1Â¾ cups unbleached flour
  • 1 cup pecans, chopped. (NOTE: I purchase pecan halves and toast them for 5-7 minutes at 350 before chopping.)

Bread Instructions

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Grease a loaf pan with butter.

Place the dates in a small bowl and sprinkle with the baking soda. Add the boiling orange juice and orange zest. Let stand for 30 minutes./p>

Using an electric mixer, beat the sugar, egg and butter until smooth.

Add the salt, cinnamon, nutmet, flour and nuts and mix on low until just blended.

Stir in the dates-soda-orange juice mixture.

Pour into greased loaf pan.

Bake for 45-50 minutes until tester inserted in bread's center comes out clean.

Allow to cool 10 minutes before turning out on a rack. Slice when cooled. (I like to cool the bread, wrap, and refrigerate over night. Slice once you remove from the refrigerator.)

Spread Ingredients

  • 8 oz cream cheese at room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon confectioners sugar
  • Zest from 1 orange
  • 2 tablespoons fresh-squeeze orange juice

Spread Instructions

Beat all ingredients until fluffy. Refrigerate until you're ready to use. Warm to room temperature before serving.
